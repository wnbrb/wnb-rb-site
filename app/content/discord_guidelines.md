# WNB.rb Discord Guidelines
The WNB.rb Discord workspace is a thriving community of more than 700 Rubyists. As WNB.rb continues to grow, it is important to adhere to a shared etiquette for interacting as an online community. This will enable our members to use our Discord workspace effectively. As a sign of respect to your fellow WNB.rb community members, please follow the guidelines listed below.

### Guidelines:

## Find the correct channel for your message
Not every message will be relevant to every member of our group, and that’s okay! When posting a new message, please look through the list of channels in our Discord workspace and choose one that is most appropriate. Every channel has a description explaining its purpose, which you can find by clicking the name of the channel on the upper-left side of the screen.

## Start public discussions
Posting in a public channel can be daunting, especially when asking for help or advice. In those cases, it might be tempting to message someone 1-on-1 instead of posting publicly. While this is not a rule, we encourage anyone who is asking for help to post in a public channel if at all possible.

Posting publicly has a few benefits:

- It helps other people who might have the same question as you.
- It normalizes asking for help and fosters a culture of collaboration.

## Reply in a thread
If you would like to reply to a message in a public channel, please do so in a thread. You can start a thread by hovering over the message and pressing the icon that looks like a chat bubble, which will open up a thread on that message. We ask that you do this for multiple reasons:

-  **Threading reduces channel noise** because replies will only notify people who have already participated in the thread. Everyone else in the channel can choose whether or not to opt into the conversation
- **Threading allows multiple conversations to happen simultaneously in the same channel.** It’s hard to post a new topic in a channel where people are actively discussing something else. Threading allows others to post in the channel without interrupting an ongoing conversation.
- **Threading keeps channels neat.** It’s hard to find old messages when every channel is cluttered with chatter and replies. Threads keep our workspace organized and readable for everyone.

## Create new channels
Every member of the WNB.rb Discord workspace has the ability to create new channels. If you think of a channel that you would like to see in our workspace, you are welcome to create it!

A few notes:

- Please first browse the channel list to make sure a similar channel doesn’t already exist (or just ask the organizers in the #ask-the-organizers channel!)
- Once you create the channel, please announce it in #new-channels! There are other people in our community who would love to join your new channel.

## Ask for help in #ask-the-organizers
Have a question about Discord or anything else relating to WNB.rb? Feel free to reach out in the #ask-the-organizers channel. One of the WNB.rb organizers will answer your question and get you on the right track. You can leave the channel as soon as your question is answered and re-join whenever you need to!

If you have a sensitive question and would not like to post it publicly, feel free to send a Direct Message to any WNB.rb organizer.

## Share each other’s (and your own!) wins
Do you know someone in the WNB.rb community who just started a new job, got a promotion, or had their talk accepted to a conference? We want to hear about it! Feel free to post shout-outs to yourself or other community members (with their permission) in the #celebrating-success channel. For example, if Emily were starting a new job today:

*Shout out to @Emily for starting her new job today! You’re going to do great!*

## Ask before cross-posting someone else’s message
WNB.rb community members often write thoughtful Discord messages, and you may want to cross-post these messages to another platform, such as a different Discord group or LinkedIn. Before doing so, please ask the author of the post whether they would be comfortable with your doing so, as well as whether they would like to be attributed.

Everyone in our community should follow this rule, including the WNB.rb organizers. The WNB.rb organizers will not cross-post any Discord message to the WNB.rb Twitter (or other social media accounts) without the author’s permission.

If you would like something crossposted to the WNB.rb Twitter account, please share it in the #twitterverse channel.

## Don’t use @channel
When someone uses @channel in a public channel, their message will notify everyone in the channel, whether or not they are currently online. With that in mind, please **never** use @channel.

If you believe your message is important enough to warrant a notification (e.g. you’re announcing a meeting or a time-sensitive survey), please select the most appropriate channel for your message and use @here.

If you’re not sure whether your message warrants an @here, feel free to ask in #ask-the-organizers -- we’d be happy to help you out!

## Reporting and Enforcement
Our goal in writing this document is NOT to punish or shame anyone. Nobody will be kicked out of the group or otherwise punished for accidentally violating one of these guidelines*. Rather, this document should serve as a communication tool to help every member of the WNB.rb Discord be respectful of others in our online space.

It is the role of the WNB.rb organizers to enforce these guidelines. We will do our best to ensure that everyone is following them and intervene when necessary. If you see repeated violations and want to bring it to the organizers’ attention, please DM any of the organizers.

*Continuous, purposeful violation of certain guidelines (e.g. somebody repeatedly using @channel in an attempt to disrupt the group) could be considered harassment and may be grounds for removal from WNB.rb, as per [our code of conduct](/code-of-conduct).*
